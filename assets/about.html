近期项目 Recent Projects

👀 LookAhead

A new direction for detecting DeFi attacks that focuses on identifying adversarial contracts instead of adversarial transactions.

Our paper →

  • A comprehensive dataset of adversarial and benign smart contracts with a variety of features pre-extracted, pre-processed and attached to each item.
  • A custom designed smart contract bytecode lifting and analysis pipeline with integrated semantic tokenization protocol that is able to efficiently extract and generate features and PSCFT for transformer and classifier training and fine-tuning.
  • An ML-based system consisting of two open-weights classifiers and one meta classifier model that is pre-trained, fine-tunable, and capable of conducting accurate and just-in-time detection of potential adversarial attacks, even when private adversarial transactions are employed by attackers.
  • The implementation and a detailed evaluation as well as performance analysis of LookAhead's detection system and methods.

🧬 NEXUS

The first non-interactive protocol for secure transformer inference.

Our paper → (To appear in NDSS'25)
Open-source implementation →

  • We propose an amortized-friendly matrix multiplication algorithm, which achieves a 1.6-3.3x speedup and saves 60% communication overhead compared to SOTA techniques.
  • We present a novel Argmax algorithm that reduces the computational complexity from O(m) in Phoenix (CCS'22) to O(logm), achieving a 55.6x speedup (m is the number of labels, m = 30522 in BERT-base).
  • We provide an end-to-end implementation and evaluation of results. NEXUS outperforms BOLT (Oakland'24) by over an order of magnitude and is 1.8x faster, 2.4x cheaper than Bumblebee (NDSS'25).
  • We also provide a GPU accelerated version of our work, which further improves the inference speed by 42.3x and reduces financial cost by 17.2x to a per-token price of only $0.05.
